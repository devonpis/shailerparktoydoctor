Work in progress

The repair

According to the owner, this giant smurf is 40 years old. The owner tried to clean him, but brown powdery material started to leak out after the wash. We found that the brown powdery substance was actually the old stuffing being decomposed and kept leaking out of the fabric. In order to rescue this smurf, we needed to thoroughly remove the old stuffing, which was not as easy as it sounded as the powdery substance all got stuck into the fabric and the fur. We opened up the smurf and turned it completely inside out. We did a thorough vacuuming, followed by cleaning, re-stuffing with new material and re-sealing. Finally this 40 years old smurf was back to his glory.

About this item

Vintage licensed Peyo Smurf giant plush (client: ~40 years old; likely late 1970s–1980s manufacture). Classic Smurf design: blue faux fur body, white Phrygian cap and pants, large round nose. Era-typical licensees for large plush included Wallace Berrie & Co. (USA), Peyo BP Australia, and European makers; giant sizes from ~28 cm to 50+ cm appear in collector listings (20 in / ~51 cm Wallace Berrie examples documented). Original fill often polyurethane foam or early polyester—breaks down to brown powder that adheres to pile (matches repair: decomposed stuffing after wash). Materials: plush pile fabric shell, internal foam/fiberfill. Not the small Schleich PVC figurines—this is stuffed plush. Market: vintage Peyo plush collectible; value depends on size, label, and condition. Confirm maker/date from sewn tag if present.
