The repair

The blue Tomy Big Loader power unit would not drive on the track — a common failure on these 1970s–90s sets when hair and grit pack the gearbox and battery tabs oxidize.

We disassembled the chassis, cleaned the wheels and reversing gearbox, refreshed the motor leads and switch, and bench-tested forward travel before reassembly.

About this item

Single Tomy Big Loader motorized power chassis (Japanese Tomy; also sold as Big Big Loader and regional Thomas/Postman Pat branded sets). Dark blue/teal squat four-wheel unit—forward arrow on top (track travel direction), front bumper peg, internal reversing gearbox driving knobby black wheels; AA battery (one or two depending on revision—1977 US orange chassis vs later Thailand-made variants). Powers the loop track for loader/dump accessories. Era: 1977–1990s popular battery toy. Market: vintage Tomy collectible; repair usually motor, contacts, or gear lint. Pair of white shell bodies for the same system: see project 0093. Sources: Big Loader collector guides.
