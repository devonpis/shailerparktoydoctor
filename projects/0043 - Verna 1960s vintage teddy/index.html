The repair

Verna is a golden vintage teddy with mohair pile that had held decades of love — the stuffing inside was decomposing and the body had holes and tears that needed careful work. We opened the bear and removed the old fill, then deep-cleaned the mohair and lining. Fabric repairs were worked through the body, the glass eyes were removed for polishing and set back securely, and stitching at the nose and mouth was reinforced. The leather paw pads were reconditioned and repainted where wear had dulled the finish. Fresh stuffing went back in and the bear was closed so Verna sits upright with her original charm restored.

About this item

Verna is a named 1960s vintage teddy bear in the post-war plush tradition — golden mohair pile on cotton backing, five-point jointing (head, arms, legs) typical of mid-century gift and collector bears. The golden colour and mohair texture match the era when regional makers still used natural-style pile before full synthetics dominated. Construction usually combines disk or cotter-pin joints, wood-wool or polyester fill (often replaced during restoration), and glass or early plastic eyes depending on maker. Verna-style named bears bridge older German/British teddy patterns and 1960s safer-child construction. Collector value depends on provenance, joint tightness, originality of pads and eyes, and condition of the mohair; documented 1960s named bears with intact character appeal are sought after in the nostalgia market.
