The repair

Homer arrived in his Santa outfit with the movement mechanism failing — worn gears, a broken rubber belt, and cracked supports at the arm and shoulder. We disassembled the figure, removed the Santa costume for a deep clean, and opened the gearbox. New gears were sourced and fitted where originals had stripped, the rubber belt in the moving mechanism was replaced, and broken arm supports plus the head attachment joint were repaired. The head received a repaint where finish had chipped, then costume, chassis, and electronics were reassembled and tested so Homer animates reliably again.

About this item

Santa Homer Simpson animatronics are licensed seasonal tie-in collectibles combining The Simpsons branding with Christmas novelty motion toys. Most examples date to 2000s-era character-merch waves where pop-culture mascots were adapted into festive decor. Typical construction includes molded plastic components, textile Santa costume elements, and a battery-operated motor/sound assembly. Market value is strongest for clearly licensed, fully functional units with intact paint and costume parts, particularly in original packaging.
