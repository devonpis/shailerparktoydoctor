A well-loved large yellow plush dog, a companion for more than ten years, came in flat and dusty with a head stain and crumbled styrofoam fill. We deep cleaned the yellow velour, lifted the stain, and restuffed with fresh polyester so he sits soft and even again.

Work in progress

The repair

This dog had been a cuddly companion for more than ten years, so the yellow pile had soil, matting, and a noticeable dark stain on the head. The old styrofoam fill had broken down into crumbs, leaving the body lumpy and flat until it was emptied out. We deep cleaned the fabric, worked on stain removal, replaced the stuffing with fresh polyester fibre, and closed the seams so the plush holds a smooth sitting pose again.

About this item

This is a large generic yellow plush dog in a cartoon sitting pose — bright yellow short-pile or velour body, floppy black fabric ears, a black oval nose, small black plastic eyes, and circular black paw pads on the feet. That colour block is common on mass-market nursery and gift plush from the 1990s through 2000s (import lines and department-store assortments) without tying it to one licensed character. Construction is usually a sewn plush shell, polyester or mixed fill, and a hidden closure along the belly or back seam. Identify by size, fabric hand, ear shape, and any surviving sewn tag rather than a single branded SKU.
